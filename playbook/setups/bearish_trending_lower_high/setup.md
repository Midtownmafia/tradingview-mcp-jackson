# Setup 3: Bearish Trending Profile Lower High Continuation
**Type:** Trend Continuation  
**Difficulty:** Beginner/Intermediate — cleaner entry, tighter stop, trend is your friend  
**Instrument:** COMEX_MINI:MGC1! (Micro Gold Futures) — 1 Minute Chart  
**Session:** London Kill Zone (2:00am–5:00am ET) preferred — any session with clear trend  
**First documented:** April 8, 2026 — MGC1! ~3:30am ET  

---

## Why This Setup Is Safer Than Setup 1 and 2

- You are **NOT counter-trading** — you are joining an established trend
- The stop is **tight and well-defined** (just above the lower high)
- The **profile shape confirms the bias** — trending profiles signal continuation, not reversal
- No waiting through absorption or timing a spike reversal
- The lower high structure gives you a clear line in the sand — above it = wrong, below it = right

---

## Core Concept

A bearish trending profile means sellers are in control — POC is near the top, thin volume below, price is moving down and away from the heavy volume node. After the initial move down, price will pull back (dead cat bounce). That bounce will fail to reach the prior high, forming a **lower high** — smart money capping the recovery. Entry is on the break of the lower high structure. The LVN below accelerates the next leg.

---

## How to Identify a Bearish Trending Profile

- Volume profile histogram is **skewed/asymmetric** — heavy volume at the TOP, thin volume below
- **NOT a balanced bell curve** (that would be a rotational market)
- **POC is in the top 30% of the profile range** — most business was done at higher prices
- Price is moving DOWN and away from the heavy volume node
- The thin area below the POC = the LVN = the fast lane for the next leg down

## Setup Anatomy

### Phase 1 — Initial Move Down + Liquidity Sweep
- Price makes a significant high (liquidity grab / spike above a level)
- Immediately reverses with strong bearish momentum
- Volume on the down bars is heavier than volume on prior up bars
- Trending profile shape begins to form — POC migrating toward the top of the range

### Phase 2 — Dead Cat Bounce
- After the initial leg down, price retraces upward
- **This bounce is the setup forming** — watch it carefully
- Bounce bars should have LOWER volume than the selling bars
- Bounce should feel weak — small bodies, wicks on tops, no conviction
- The bounce approaches (but does NOT reach) the prior swing high

### Phase 3 — Lower High Confirmation
- Price stalls below the prior swing high — **lower high is confirmed**
- Lower high coincides with a resistance level:
  - Prior session VAH
  - Prior POC now acting as resistance
  - Key horizontal ray (Weekly/Overnight/PDaily level)
- The lower high candle shows: small body, upper wick, close near the low
- 5m and 15m momentum is turning bearish again

### Phase 4 — Entry Trigger
- **ENTRY: 1m close BELOW the lower high candle's low**
- This is the break of the lower high structure — momentum resuming down
- Do NOT enter on the lower high candle itself — wait for the break

### Phase 5 — LVN Acceleration
- Price enters the LVN below — accelerates through thin volume area
- Strong volume bars resume the downtrend
- Target is the next HVN below, then the liquidity pool below that

## Entry, Stop, Target

| Parameter | Level | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Entry** | 1m close below the lower high candle's low | Break of structure |
| **Stop (tight)** | Above the lower high wick | 5–10 ticks typically |
| **Stop (standard)** | Above the lower high + 2 ticks buffer | Gives a little room |
| **Target 1** | MTF POC cluster | Partial / move stop to BE |
| **Target 2** | Liquidity pool below (prior lows / VAL cluster) | Full target |
| **Target 3** | Next key level (Weekly/Overnight/PDaily) | Runner |

**R:R note:** Stop is very tight (5–10 ticks above lower high wick). Target is the prior swing low or session VP level below — often 30–80 ticks away. This is the best R:R of all four setups.

## Alert Criteria (When to Get Ready)

Alert fires when ALL of the following are true:
1. Trending profile detected (POC in top 30% of profile range)
2. Price made a prior swing high (visible reference point)
3. Price is currently in a bounce phase (moving back toward prior high)
4. 4H/1H bias is bearish
5. Session more than 30 minutes old

**WATCH state — monitor for lower high to form.**

---

## Entry Criteria (When to Pull the Trigger)

Enter SHORT when ALL of the following are true:
1. Price rallied to within 10–20 ticks of prior swing high BUT closed below it = **lower high confirmed**
2. Lower high coincides with a known resistance level (prior VAH, POC, or horizontal ray)
3. Lower high candle shows: small body, upper wick, bearish close
4. Bounce volume was noticeably lower than the initial selling volume
5. 5m and 15m momentum turning bearish (bearish bodies forming)
6. Next 1m bar closes below the lower high candle's low = **entry trigger**
7. Clear liquidity pool / target visible below = you know where you're going

---

## Take Profit Criteria

**Partial / Target 1 (MTF POC):**
- Close half at MTF POC
- Move stop to breakeven on remainder

**Full / Target 2 (Liquidity pool / session VP level):**
- Prior swing lows, VAL cluster, or session VP level below
- Exit if 5m candle closes back above 5m VAH (momentum lost)

**Trail stop rule:**
- Once T1 hit, trail stop to swing high of each bounce
- Exit immediately if price closes above the lower high (structure broken)

---

## Stop Management

- Initial stop: above the lower high wick (the tightest valid stop in the playbook)
- If price immediately accelerates: trail stop to swing high of each subsequent bounce
- Hard exit: price closes ABOVE the prior swing high = lower high structure broken, thesis wrong

---

## Key Observations / Lessons

1. **The tight stop is what makes this setup special**: 5–10 ticks above the lower high wick gives you a defined, small risk with a large potential reward. Protect this by not moving your stop wider.

2. **Profile shape is the first filter**: if the profile is balanced (bell curve), this is NOT a trending setup. Do not take Setup 3 in a balanced market — take Setup 1 instead.

3. **The lower high must be below the prior high**: if price takes out the prior high, the bearish trend structure is broken. Exit immediately and reassess.

4. **Volume confirmation matters**: bounce volume should be clearly lower than selling volume. If bounce volume is equal or higher, buyers are absorbing the sellers — be cautious.

5. **The LVN below is your tailwind**: trending profiles have LVNs below the POC. Once price enters the LVN, it moves fast. Don't micro-manage the trade — let it run to the next HVN.

---

## What Would Have Invalidated This Setup

- Price closes ABOVE the prior swing high = structure broken, trend reversing up
- Profile begins to balance (new volume building above POC) = trending shape breaking down
- Bounce forms but has equal/greater volume than the selling bars = buyers absorbing, not a dead cat
- Lower high forms with no coinciding resistance level = lower conviction, skip or reduce size
- 4H candle is indecision / doji — no clear bias
- Kill zone less than 30 minutes old
